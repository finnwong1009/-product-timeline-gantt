# Launch Planning Reference

## Default Launch Phases

| Phase | Typical Nodes | Gate Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Strategy | market/context review, target segment, positioning, business goal | Are we solving the right problem for the right audience? |
| Definition | PRD, scope, metrics, dependency map, launch assumptions | Is the product scope clear enough to execute? |
| Build | design lock, development, content/material prep, channel prep | Are product and launch workstreams moving together? |
| Validation | QA entry, beta/pilot, feedback triage, issue burn-down | Do we have evidence this is ready for users? |
| Readiness | pricing/package, legal/compliance, support, sales/channel enablement | Can every launch-facing team execute on time? |
| Launch | go/no-go, release, announcement, monitoring | Are owners ready for launch-day decisions? |
| Post-launch | metric review, feedback review, retrospective, iteration plan | Did the launch meet goals and what changes next? |

## Backward Planning Template

Start from launch day and work backward:

| Relative Time | Node | Deliverable | Owner | Dependency | Gate |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| T-8w | Strategy lock | positioning, target audience, launch objective | PM/strategy | market input | leadership approval |
| T-6w | Scope lock | PRD, priority list, out-of-scope list | PM | design/engineering estimates | committed scope |
| T-4w | Experience lock | design specs, content outline, tracking plan | design/PM/data | scope lock | implementation-ready |
| T-3w | QA entry | test plan, build handoff, known issue list | engineering/QA | feature complete | QA accepted |
| T-2w | Beta/readiness | beta feedback, enablement draft, risk register | PM/ops/support | usable build | no critical blockers |
| T-1w | Launch approval | go/no-go doc, rollout plan, comms package | PM/launch owner | readiness signoff | launch approved |
| T | Launch | release, announcement, monitoring dashboard | launch team | approval | live and monitored |
| T+1w | Review | metrics, feedback, issues, iteration proposal | PM/data | launch data | next actions agreed |

## Planning Quality Checklist

- Objective is measurable and connected to business or user value.
- Target user and scenario are specific, not generic.
- Scope includes explicit exclusions.
- Every launch node has an owner and gate.
- Timing accounts for review cycles, not only production time.
- Dependencies across product, engineering, design, data, legal, content, sales, support, and channels are visible.
- Risks include mitigation and escalation owner.
- Metrics include leading indicators, launch-day monitoring, and post-launch success measures.
- Open decisions are named with decision owner and deadline.
